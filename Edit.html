Example

Filters

  1. From: A date with time representing the start of your query time range. No information older than this date is used.
  2. To: A date with time representing the end of your query time range. No information newer than this date is used.
  3. Channel Types: The channel types to filter by. When channel types are provided only conversations with those channels will be in the result. Specifying none will mean all channel types are included.

  5. Dispositions: Specific dispositions that must occur in the resulting conversations. Multiple dispositions can be present in a conversation. Specifying none will mean all dispositions are included.
  6. Users: Specific users that must occur in the resulting conversations. Multiple users can be present on a conversation. Specifying none will mean all users are included.
  7. Contact Method: The contact method relevant to the conversation. A telephone number or an email for example. Using this filter allows one to view conversations from a specific contact. Specifying none will mean all contact methods are included.
  8. Direction: The direction of the conversation. Using this filter allows one to view specifically inbound/outbound interactions containing conversations.
  9. Keywords: This filter allows you to search for specific keywords related to a conversation. For example, keywords in the body of an email.
  10. Tags: Display conversations data that have had specific Tags linked. Conversations can be Tagged when the Interaction is open on the screen.
  11. Groups: Specific User Groups, you wish the information to be limited to. Selecting Group ‘A’ and ‘B’ for example will result in data related to those groups. User groups are created in settings and associated with the User Profiles. Specifying no group means all groups will be included.
  12. Duration: Set a duration to view conversations that continued longer than a specific amount of time.
  13. Channels: Specific Channels you wish the information to be limited to. Selecting Channel ‘A’ and ‘B’ for example will result in data related to those Channels. Specifying no Channel means all Channels will be included.
  14. Companies: Specific companies you wish the information to be limited to. Selecting companies’ ‘A’ and ‘B’ for example will result in data related to those companies. Specifying no company means all companies will be included. Note that the company filter is the last filter to be applied to the report. Should the data result be more than 100 records before applying the Company filter, the UI will only display data that is available in the first 1000 records. To see all the data for 1000 records or more, you will need to export the report to CSV. CSV export is limited to 100 000 records.
  15. Fields: Filter for the exported contact entity fields. By default, all are provided.
  16. Exclude Departments: Specific departments the information should exclude from the data. Selecting departments ‘A’ and ‘B’ for example will result in data related to those departments being excluded from the data. Specifying no departments means no departments will be excluded.
  17. Use Time Only: By selecting this option, you can use the date range selection to search a date range specifying specific times instead of complete From and To date ranges and times. This will filter the report to show conversations from the 1st of July 2022 to the 10th of July 2022 that only occurred between 16:00:00 and 17:00:00 across those dates.
  18. Sentiment: Allows you to filter and view conversations based on the categorized emotional tone (positive, negative, or neutral) detected in each interaction.

Output

  1. Started: The time the conversation initially started.
  2. Duration: The duration of the conversation being live.
  3. First Agent Time: The time the conversation was actually answered by the user. After the Started time of the conversation.
  4. Total Agent Time: The total time the user spent on the conversation.
  5. Departments: The departments the conversation went through.
  6. Users: The users that were offered the conversation or were active participants in the conversation.
  7. Dispositions: The dispositions applied to the conversation.
  8. Contact Methods: The contact methods of the contacts involved with the conversation.
  9. Referral Source: The referral source from which the conversation spawned, if any were used.
  10. CSAT: Customer satisfaction rating. The CSAT rating is from 1-5 Stars, where 1 is the lowest rating and 5 is the highest rating.
